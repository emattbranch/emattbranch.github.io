Research methods

  • Quantitative research design
  • Qualitative methods (contextual inquiry, surveys & questionnaires)
  • Eye tracking
  • Statistical analysis (descriptive, inferential, data graphing)

See more

I have studied research design including observational, correlational, and experimental. Although observational has been the most applicable to my design projects so far, knowing how to ensure a research study is experimentally valid has improved my research skills in all areas. Balancing control of variables with ecological validity is critical and plays into every study design.

Observational studies I have completed include task analysis, contextual inquiry, and surveys. Each of these requires interpreting participant responses to make valid design choices. Quantitative measures can help with interpreting results; I have often used a combination of the two in a single study. Eye tracking is a great example, as interview questions are necessary to interpret the metrics.

In addition, I have studied descriptive and inferential statistics through dependent samples t-Test and one-way ANOVA.

Psychology study

  • Fundamentals of neurodesign
  • Fundamentals of persuasive design
  • Accessibility evaluation

See more

I have greatly enjoyed every opportunity to study the interaction of psychology and design in my graduate program. I find the relatively new field of Neurodesign to be fascinating. Through these classes I have studied the overall structure and function of the brain, including different mechanisms such as the strong connection between memory and emotion, and the biological workings of cognitive load. This knowledge is especially useful for persuasive design; I have learned that every design choice has an influence on the end user's decisions. I strive to be an ethical designer who avoids manipulating users or creating negative psychological effects beyond the design itself.

Accessibility can sometimes seem like a separate field than design, but I believe it is essential to creating a universally positive experience. For example, applying above average contrast not only ensures that contrast-impaired people have access, but improves the experience for everyone. I have completed in depth accessibility analysis in a few projects using the browser WAV tool in conjunction with W3C accessibility standards. There is always more that can be done to improve in this area, and I aim to make it a central focus of my work.
